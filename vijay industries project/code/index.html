Distribution Transformers

A distribution transformer is a type of transformer that performs the final voltage conversion in the electric power distribution system, reducing the high voltage from the primary distribution lines to the low, safe voltage level used by residential, commercial, and small industrial customers.

View Details →

Power Transformers

A power transformer is a static electrical device used in the high-voltage transmission and sub-transmission segments of the electric power grid. Its primary function is to efficiently change the voltage level between different parts of the network, typically operating at constant full load.

Step-Up Transformers

A step-up transformer is an electrical device that uses electromagnetic induction to increase (step up) the voltage from the primary (input) side to the secondary (output) side. Simultaneously, it decreases the current to maintain power conservation (ignoring losses).

View Details →

Step-Down Transformers

A step-down transformer is an electrical device that uses electromagnetic induction to decrease (step down) the voltage from the primary (input) side to the secondary (output) side.

View Details →

Isolation Transformers

An isolation transformer is a type of transformer specifically designed to transfer electrical power from an AC source to a device or circuit while providing galvanic isolation between the two circuits.

View Details →

Control Transformers

A control transformer is a specialized type of step-down transformer designed to provide a stable, low-voltage power supply for the control circuits of industrial equipment and machinery.
